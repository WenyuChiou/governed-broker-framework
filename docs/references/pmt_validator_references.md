## Core Theoretical References

### PMT Original

Rogers, R. W. (1983). Cognitive and physiological processes in fear appeals and attitude change: A revised theory of protection motivation. In J. T. Cacioppo & R. E. Petty (Eds.), *Social psychophysiology: A sourcebook* (pp. 153-176). Guilford Press.

- **Type**: Book Chapter
- **Key Contribution**: Original PMT framework with Threat Appraisal + Coping Appraisal
- **Used For**: R2 (LOW TP + relocate = Error), R3 theoretical basis

---

### PMT Flood Extension

Grothmann, T., & Reusswig, F. (2006). People at risk of flooding: Why some residents take precautionary action while others do not. *Natural Hazards*, 38(1-2), 101-120. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11069-005-8604-6

- **DOI**: 10.1007/s11069-005-8604-6
- **Type**: Empirical (Germany, N=157 household interviews)
- **Key Findings**:
  - HIGH TP + HIGH CP → Protection Motivation → Action
  - HIGH TP + LOW CP → Non-protective responses (denial/fatalism)
  - TP × CP interaction effects
- **Used For**: R1 (HIGH TP + HIGH CP + do_nothing = Error)
- **Citation Count**: 1500+ (as of 2024)

---

### Meta-Analysis

Bamberg, S., Masson, T., Brewitt, K., & Nemetschek, N. (2017). Threat, coping and flood prevention – A meta-analysis. *Journal of Environmental Psychology*, 54, 116-126. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jenvp.2017.08.001

- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jenvp.2017.08.001
- **Type**: Meta-analysis (35 studies, 47 samples, N=35,419)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Coping Appraisal: r = 0.30 (stronger predictor)
  - Threat Appraisal: r = 0.23
- **Used For**: R4 (LOW CP → expensive action = Error)
- **Corrigendum**: Published December 2019

---

### PADM

Lindell, M. K., & Perry, R. W. (2012). The Protective Action Decision Model: Theoretical modifications and additional evidence. *Risk Analysis*, 32(4), 616-632. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1539-6924.2011.01647.x

- **DOI**: 10.1111/j.1539-6924.2011.01647.x
- **Type**: Theoretical + Empirical Review
- **Key Findings**:
  - Three core perceptions: Threat, Protective Action, Stakeholder
  - Stakeholder perception affects protective action decisions
- **Used For**: R4, R5 (SP-related rules)

## Flood Experience → Risk Perception Studies

### Germany (Saxony) Study

Bubeck, P., Botzen, W. J. W., & Aerts, J. C. J. H. (2012). A review of risk perceptions and other factors that influence flood mitigation behavior. *Risk Analysis*, 32(9), 1481-1495. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1539-6924.2011.01783.x

- **DOI**: 10.1111/j.1539-6924.2011.01783.x
- **Type**: Empirical (N=2,200+ residents)
- **Key Finding**: Flood experience → increased threat perception
- **Used For**: R3 (Flood occurred + claims safe = Error)

### New Zealand (Hutt Valley) Study

Lawrence, J., Quade, D., & Becker, J. (2014). Integrating the effects of flood experience on risk perception with responses to changing climate risk. *Natural Hazards*, 74(3), 1773-1794. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11069-014-1288-z

- **Type**: Mixed methods (surveys, workshops, interviews)
- **Key Finding**: Prior flood experience → heightened risk perception + preparedness
- **Used For**: R3
