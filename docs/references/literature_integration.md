## Key Findings Integration

### Finding 1: Coping Appraisal > Threat Appraisal
- **International:** Bamberg (2017) r=0.30 vs r=0.23
- **US:** Botzen NYC (2019), Botzen FL (2024)
- **Consensus:** Coping is stronger predictor across all contexts

### Finding 2: Salience Effect Confirmed
- **International:** Choi (2024) +7% insurance uptake
- **US:** Choi (2024) county-level, Botzen FL (2024) real-time
- **Consensus:** Flood experience increases threat perception, but decays

### Finding 3: Insurance-Mitigation Complementarity
- **International:** Rufat (2024) NYC
- **US:** Rufat (2024) NYC, Botzen (2019) NYC
- **Consensus:** Insurance does NOT substitute for physical measures

### Finding 4: Place Attachment Barrier
- **International:** Limited evidence
- **US:** Bukvic (2023) N=1,450, Bukvic (2022) index
- **Consensus:** Place attachment reduces relocation willingness

### Finding 5: Environmental Justice
- **International:** Limited evidence
- **US:** Collins (2019), Billings (2022)
- **Consensus:** Low-SES and minority households disproportionately affected

### Finding 6: Financial Capacity Critical
- **International:** Bamberg (2017) meta-analysis
- **US:** Billings (2022) bankruptcies, Rufat (2024)
- **Consensus:** Affordability is decisive for expensive measures

Our PMT consistency validators are grounded in 34 empirical studies spanning
9 countries/regions with over 90,000 participants (2010-2024). Meta-analytic
